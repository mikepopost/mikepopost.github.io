(Pronounced: Poe-Kriff-Key)

Mike “Po” Pokryfke is an Emmy Award-winning editor, motion graphics artist, jib operator, gaffer, producer, writer, and director of photography. He’s worked on short films, music videos, live events, commercials, and run-on sentences in the Chicago area. He loves coffee, Twizzlers and justice. He hates tomatoes. He is a father of 3 (2 boys and a girl, all under the age of 7) and thus prone to little sleep.

Mike graduated from DePaul University’s School of Computing and Digital Media with a Bachelor of Arts in Digital Cinema, where his primary focus was on production lighting and cinematography. The summer before his senior year he took an interest in the post-production side of things and began to familiarize himself with a variety software and techniques, mainly teaching himself via online tutorials.

After graduation, Mike gained valuable experience at a boutique marketing agency assuming a multitude of roles for different client projects. Soon after, he landed at WGN-TV (Chicago), as an editor/producer in the Creative Services department. There he spent his time writing and cutting promos for the retro television network, Antenna TV, as well as assisting in the re-launch of the free over-the-air movie network, THIS-TV.

In 2014, with baby #1 on the way, Mike left his position at WGN-TV (& surrendered his title as reigning Fantasy Football Champion) to pursue freelance work. It was then that he began working with FOUR Productions, a small Chicago-based production company, as director of post-production. At FOUR, Mike spent just as much time on-set as he did in the edit suite, working as a camera operator, jib operator, or just being overbearing to make sure he had enough coverage to make a solid edit. Nobody else at FOUR played fantasy football.

In 2017, Mike accepted a position as the Executive Producer of the Video Department at WGN Radio AM720 in Chicago. He handled projects from ideation to completion for WGN Radio's sales clients, while creating supplemental video content for the shows on-air. In addition to that, he generated original station content such as the “Goodbuy Girl” and guest interviews in the PPG Paints Green Room.

In January of 2020, Mike was given the opportunity to transfer to the cable network WGN America, where he handled the promotion of the WGN America original series "Almost Paradise." After one season, the network pivoted to a primetime news format launching "NewsNation." Mike currently handles writing, production, and editing of many NewsNation commercials you may see on-air today.

Mike has also worked as a freelance jib operator for concerts (Ice Cube, Common, Bobby Brown, Company of Thieves), music videos (King Louie), and corporate shoots (Chicago Blackhawks “Monuments to Excellence” and “Hat Trick”).

When he is not working, you can find Mike playing guitar, videogames, or woodworking in his garage. Occasionally, he produces other content for fun with his friends under the banner Gorilla House Films.

Mike also put googly eyes on Lady Edith from Downton Abbey a few times.
